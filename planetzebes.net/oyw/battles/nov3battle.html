Persephone Dawn Spars Caleb DeGrau


3 days from Earth, a perpetual no man's land incorporating most of the densely populated (dense once, anyhow), Luna, and L3. The third ring of five, separating two malevolent parties from squashing the other beneath its boot heel, is what one would call the center of the Earth Sphere-aside from Earth, of course. Stars shine in all dimensions, the lunar plane the only major contender in this particular region. Just now it spins around the Earth, bringing the tides along with it.

A Zaku, peculiar in nature, was speeding somewhere-no where-but suddenly stops. Persephone Dawn, still struck by the stellar beauty of space cannot help herself. Having spent little time away from Granada, this was a treat. Surely, good things would come of this.

As if to mark the occasion, Caleb DeGrau, SAF toughguy, ruined the moment by appearing moonward and opening a communication channel. "Hey, so, I was told you'd be here."

"I knew you'd be here." Dawn said flatly, not really paying attention.

"Let's just do this. Do it or lose it."

The Zaku with more arms than really necessary, lunged, torso forward (mimicking something that had occurred not too long ago), but instead of a kiss, the Zaku planted a sharp jab into the Gelgoog's right pauldron with one of its clawed arms. It tore a sizable chunk from the awkwardly green armor. DeGrau was quite clearly stunned by the maneuverâ€”having been the aggressor all his life, it wasn't too often someone beat him to the chase.

Instead of gawking all day, he unloaded 90mm rounds into the Zaku's torso, creating hollowing tinking noises despite the emptiness of space. Dawn retreats, still unphased. Her reaction time seems uncanny, and DeGrau cursed at how little he's hitting her now. The ungainly Zaku stayed in the Gelgoog's blind spot, peppering its pray at every turn.

Finally, DeGrau's had enough. He stopped dead. Dawn, still jinking and diving from before, overshot just a hair and the Gelgoog grabbed hold of one of her manipulator arms--more luck than skill. The thing seemed to writhe in anguish as DeGrau swung it into his vulcan's firing arc and let loose. 50mm rounds struck the Zaku from head to toe.

"Hey babe, this ain't no Grenade-a," sneered DeGrau, poking fun at what little he knew about her.

Dawn ignored him.
